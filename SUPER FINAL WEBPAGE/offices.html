Facilities Office

General Information

The Facilities Office (FO) provides and maintains an ideal academic, physical setting for the Institute. Its main function is to create and maintain a safe environment for students and associates.

The office also monitors the use of the physical facilities, building equipment, as well as the activities or events within the campus premises.

Our Services

Cleaning, Disinfecting, & Sanitizing

The Facilities Office provides and maintains the cleanliness and orderliness inside the campus. Disinfection and sanitation are done regularly to prevent the spread of infection and virus, especially in high touchpoint and high-traffic areas. Daily cleaning routines (including vacuuming, sweeping, scrubbing, dust and dirt removal, pest control, and proper waste disposal) are strictly observed to ensure a clean and safe environment for students and associates.

Equipment Maintenance

It ensures that all machineries and equipment are in good running condition by scheduling regular preventive maintenance.

Security

It secures the assets of the Institute, maintains peace and order inside the premises, and always ensures the safety of its faculty, associates, and students.

Safety & Health

FO maintains a safe and healthy place for Tamaraws. It conducts a safety and health management program in coordination with the Health Service Unit Department to focus efforts in improving work and school environment, prevent injuries and illnesses, identify hazards, and discuss how to manage the safety and health of everyone in the school.

Visitor Management

It provides and maintains a record of all visitors who are coming to the campus. Entry and exit assessment by ID verification is done through providing badges for parents, students, associates, alumni, concessionaires, contractors, and other visitors who have campus transactions. Also, the office conducts a contact tracing process through the Stay Safe application which helps in reducing the spread of the COVID-19.

On-Campus Activity Program Management

FO provides and maintains a good facility and readiness of rooms including the conference room, multi-purpose room, student plaza, cafeteria, chapel, piazza, and gym. It is in charge of preparing these areas for any scheduled event of the faculty and students. The office also provides sound system, furniture, and manpower assistance for all kinds of activities inside the campus.

Vehicle

It also ensures readiness of the company vehicle for any official business trip requested by the faculty, students, and other offices that may need to do a specific assigned task.

CCTV Reviews

The Facilities Office ensures the functionality and operation of CCTV systems in the whole area. It assists students and associates in getting CCTV reviews and reports in coordination with the Human Resources Office.

Inventory Management

FO ensures the proper encoding, tagging, tracking, and control of the inventory materials as it is bought, stored, and used. It makes sure to deliver, receive, and store the right quantities of items. The office also conducts quarterly review and physical inventory of all assets, furniture and fixtures, laboratory tools, equipment, and other materials.

Project Management

FO ensures the existing project development in the area is being accomplished and done on a timely basis. Construction works are properly executed, monitored, and controlled based on the scope of works and methodology. This includes planning, identifying tasks, quantifying the resources needed, as well as determining budgets and timelines for completion. The office also coordinates with the different vendors and contractors for the completion of project works.

Communications in Local Government and Neighborhood

It also maintains and continues good communication with its local government for the list of tasks and protocols which the campus is required to do so. It also ensures proper communication and information from the neighborhood for other necessary events that may be related to the school and community operation.

Finance Office

General Information

The Finance Office is responsible for maintaining the Institute’s books of accounts and the preparation and interpretation of financial statements. The Accounting Director exercises overall supervision of the following sections of the Finance Office namely budget, purchasing, disbursements, general accounting, and student accounts sections.

Services

Budget Section

  1. Review proposed budget submitted by Directors and prepare a master budget plan for submission to the Board.
  2. Maintain records of the budgeted and actual revenue and expenses to determine performance of various departments/offices.
  3. Determine whether requests are within the authorized budget.
  4. Determine budget deviations and discuss corrective measures with departments.

General Accounting Section

  1. Prepare trial balance and schedules.
  2. Prepare financial statements.
  3. Prepare bank reconciliations statements.
  4. Record receipts.
  5. Prepare monthly remittances to government authorities.

Students Accounts Section

  1. Maintain records of individual students.
  2. Issue examination permits.
  3. Issue statement of accounts for refund.
  4. Issue clearances for returning students.
  5. Determine amount of refund.

Disbursements Section

  1. Responsible for ensuring that all disbursements of college funds are in order and properly authorized, meaning they are properly documented, funded and approved.
  2. Ensures that authorized suppliers receive the correct payment when they are due.
  3. Prepare checks.
  4. Release checks to authorize payee.

Purchasing Section

  1. Responsible for the timely procurement and receiving of materials, goods, equipment and services.
  2. Search for suppliers.
  3. Negotiate the best price.
  4. Procure the necessary materials, equipment, or suppliers adhering to the Institute’s purchasing policies and procedures.

Human Resources

General Information

The Human Resources Office is a critical component of employee well-being in any business, no matter how small. Our responsibilities include recruitment, hiring, placement, salary and benefits administration, training and development of Associates and compliance to and keeping up to date with governing laws.

We work towards synergistic partnerships and alliances with the various Departments and Offices within the College for purposes of creating, for each and every associate, an environment of trust, continued growth and excellence. It is our belief that under this kind of environment, all of us could drive the College into the future with maximum commitment.

We believe that the primary responsibility of the Human Resources Office is to support line management with the processes, systems, and services needed to optimally manage the human capital. The overall level of people results in an organization clearly depends on the quality of this key partnership between line managers who know and accept their roles and the Human Resources Office who are committed to properly support them.


The main task of the Human Resources Office is to support the School in the achievement of its strategic objectives through:
  1. Internal consultancy and relationship building with its customers.
  2. Provision of specialist knowledge and advice both to management and associates.
  3. Evaluation, recommendation and implementation of appropriate HR interventions that address people issues and enhance associates’ morale and productivity.
  4. Increased individual and organizational performance to achieve business results.

Vision

The Human Resources is a strategic partner and trusted advisor, providing reliable, excellent service, acknowledging that everyone makes a difference. We advocate for diversity and quality work life. We maintain the highest professional standards. We are trustworthy, proactive, and objective in our work, while maintaining compassion and dignity for the individual.

Mission

The Human Resources Office is committed in helping develop the College to be an “employer of choice,” which attracts, rewards and retains highly competent and principled associates who are engaged and enabled to help shape and achieve its goals through the development and administration of cost-effective and results-oriented programs, policies, services, and practices.

Values

The Human Resources Office is committed to the following values:
  • Integrity and Honesty
    • Our belief in integrity demands that each employee conducts himself and takes decisions in accordance with the highest principles of honesty and ethical business practice.
  • Teamwork
    • Teamwork means recognizing “team” as not just the work unit an individual belongs to but also all other organizational units.

    In practice, teamwork means aligning personal interests and objectives with those of the team, and in some instances, even subordinating personal interests and objectives. It is involving concerned individuals or units in decisions and projects relevant to them. It is teaching, speaking for, and supporting one another.

  • Professionalism
    • Professionalism is manifested by taking accountability for all aspects of our duties and ensuring that these are discharged in accordance with the highest possible standards. It is recognizing that our employment in the College is a personal choice, and we must therefore provide the organization what is rightfully due in terms of performance and productivity.

    Professionalism means managing work-related differences so that all these do not affect interpersonal relationships. It is driving one’s own holistic development and continuous growth in competence. In our daily interaction with others, this means treating them with the highest norms of courtesy. It is dealing with others fairly and sincerely. It is showing proper regard to individuals’ use of time by keeping appointments, arriving at, starting and ending meetings on time. It means creating a work environment that develops and maintains a skilled, well informed and equitably rewarded workforce.

  • Concern for the Customer
    • This means identifying both internal and external customers and knowing their requirements; designing systems and policies to meet customers’ needs on a continuing basis in accordance with established service standards; and, keeping the customer informed throughout the transaction, especially if deviations occur, and being responsible for him until his needs are met.

  • Excellence with a Passion
    • Excellence is unceasingly seeking and realizing the highest professional and personal standards in all we do. It means possessing a constant positive discontent with the status quo that drives us to find a better way than what is currently accepted as “the best way”. It is constantly seeking to outdo ourselves by achieving more and better than our prior accomplishments.

    Our Services

    Talent Selection, Acquisition and Management

    Recruitment or employment services is concerned with attracting the most qualified associates and matching them to the jobs for which they are best suited. HR associates recruit (internally or externally), screen, and interview job applicants. They may also check references and extend job offers. Similarly, they conduct orientation sessions and arrange on-the-job training for new associates.

    Employment services is also concerned with managing the attrition of associates. HR associates conduct exit interviews for purposes of determining the underlying reasons for employees’ resignation. In this way, management is able to maintain the turnover rate at a reasonable level through the implementation of appropriate HR interventions.

    • Recruitment and Selection is responsible in Identifying and fulfilling talent needs.
    • Utilizing various recruitment technologies to acquire a high volume of applicants (and to filter based on experience).
    • Maintaining ethical hiring practices and aligning with the regulatory environment and prepares employees contract and negotiating salary and benefits.
    • Periodic performance assessments for strengthened commitment and continuous improvement.

    Learning and Development

    The school recognizes that training offers a way to develop skills, enhance productivity and quality of work, build associates loyalty to the Institute, and most importantly, increase individual and organizational performance to achieve business results.

    • Learning and Development conducts, and organizes training programs to improve employee performance and ensure organizational productivity.
    • The Human Resources is also guided to focus on nurturing discipline, simplicity and motivation. Learning, knowledge exchange, people development is a must in building a team while developing individuals pro-actively in the college.
    • Providing training opportunities (internal training, educational programs, conferences, etc.) to keep employees up to date in their respective fields.
    • Develop their general capabilities as individuals and discover their own potentials for their own and/or organizational development purposes.
    • Develop a culture within the Institute in which directors and associates’ relationship, team work, and collaboration among the different departments and offices are strong and contribute to their professional well-being, motivation, and pride.

    Compensation and Benefits

    Compensation & Benefits Planning and Administration pertains to the development, implementation, and administration of the Institute’s salaries and benefits policies.
    • Understanding the importance of providing competitive compensation and benefits to employees, staying current on trends to attract and retain talents.
    • Monitoring the school’s salary structure and benefits provision to ensure a balance between control of costs as well as attracting and retaining associates.
    • Managing the payroll system.
    • Developing and implementing new benefit packages while ensuring that these are current, competitive, and in line with legal requirements.
    • Negotiating group health insurance rates, retirement plans, and other benefits with third party providers.
    • Ensuring compliance with legal (government mandatory benefits) and cultural expectations when it comes to employee compensation.

    Employee Relations is dedicated to creating and sustaining a positive work environment by helping resolve work-related problems and by providing special programs to recognize and reward associates’ contributions to the Institute.
    • Handling and sustaining good employee relations, effective conflict management in the workplace that is vital in maintaining successful work teams.
    • Advice and coaching for directors and supervising associates on performance and other on-the-job problems.
    • Clarification and advice on the School’s policies pertaining to human resources as well as compliance with laws governing employment.
    • Guidance to management in interpreting the Labor Code of the Philippines, Manual of Regulations for Private Schools, and other legislation pertaining to employment.
    • Programs that recognize long service, outstanding customer service, and excellence.
    • Recreational programs that enable associates to be part of the social fabric of the Institute’s community.
