Introduction To Miles

The Mastery Individualized Learning Enhancement System (MILES) MILES stands for Mastery-based Individualized Learning Enhancement System. It is a pioneering educational system that extends the capability of Canvas* to ensure mastery and individualized learning. Mastery learning is an innovative technological strategy in education which maintains that students need to achieve a level of mastery in prerequisite topics before moving on to learn subsequent topics. It helps build a strong foundation for present and future learning. The learning environment is further enhanced by individualized learning where faculty members provide mentoring and tutoring support to individual students. With MILES, students learn and practice their lessons using well-designed content and assessments geared toward mastery of the lesson—encouraging students to reach higher levels of achievement and develop more confidence in their ability to learn. *Canvas is FEU Alabang, FEU Diliman, and FEU Tech’s Learning Management System (LMS)..

STUDENT CODE OF CONDUCT

GENERAL BEHAVIOR

Students enrolled whether in Fully Online or Blended Online should conform with the policies specified as an addendum to the existing provisions of the student code of conduct of the Student Handbook:
1. Students shall follow the Institution’s guidelines on new normal operations should they visit/attend classes in the school.
2. Students shall, at all times, conduct themselves in an appropriate decorum and appearance during video conferences and other school activities that would require video presence. This may also include a well-groomed hair style.
3. Students shall at all times and in all forms of medium, including but not limited to social media, be respectful and proper in their conduct. They shall refrain from using language and committing acts that are offensive, disrespectful, vulgar or indecent, or which in any manner may cause or disturb other students, associates and the Institution as well.
4. Students must not malign school (all FEU campuses) name, its administration, associates, faculty, and staff, in any social media platforms.
5. Students shall not engage in online gambling, lottery or in any scheme where chance, monetary and/or material considerations are involved. Such schemes include, with or without the element of chance, the enticement of the students to part with their property in consideration of an empty promise of gain or benefit.
6. Students shall abide/comply with all the instructions concerning online classes given by faculty/facilitator.
7. Students must wear any decent attire (collared shirts for male and blouse with sleeves for female students) or any FEU shirt during video conferences / synchronous class activities such that faculty member (teacher) may require them to participate.
Supplemental Handbook for Students 14 8. Students shall not smoke (using cigarette or vape) or be under the influence of liquor or prohibited drugs whenever attending synchronous online classes/ consultations which requires video appearances.
9. Students shall not eat or drink during video conferences classes/ synchronous classes.
10. Students must not post videos of their teachers/associates in any social media platform without permission from the school or the teacher/associate involved. All video recorded lectures of teachers are owned by the school.
11. Students shall not disseminate to unintended recipients all educational learning activities and materials, including assessments obtained or uploaded from MILES/Canvas, in any medium. Recording and posting of such materials in any social media platforms (without permission) shall not be permitted as well. This act shall be considered a major offense.
12. In relation to the preceding paragraph, students shall not commit any form of cheating, thus, they must submit the Pledge of Honesty.
13. Students must use their official FEU Gmail for transactions with FEU offices only.
14. Students shall abide with the penal laws applicable to academic institutions including but not limited to:
     - Anti - bullying Act
     - Cybercrime Prevention Act
     - Safe Spaces Act
18. Students who will be proven disseminating bomb jokes or any false information regarding explosives or any form of destruction will be penalized under major offense of the student handbook.
